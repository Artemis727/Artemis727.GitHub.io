Modeling

How to formalize an optimization task as a mathematical model

Given a set of Items $I$, each item $i \in I$ characterized by

  • its weight $w_i$
  • its value $v_i$

and

  • a capacity $K$ for a knapsack

find the subset of items in $I$

  • that has maximum value
  • does not exceed the capacity $K$ of the knapsack

How to model an optimization peoblem

  1. decision variables -> encode the result
  2. problem constraints in terms of those decision variables -> specify the solutions of the problems
  3. objective function -> specify the quality of each problem

The result is an optimization model

  • decalrative formulation -> ‚Äòwhat‚Äô, not ‚Äòhow‚Äô

  • many ways, many models

A knapsack Model

$$
maximize \sum_{i \in I}v_ix_i \
subject\quad to \sum_{i \in I}w_ix_i \leq K \
x_i \in {0,1} \quad (i\in I)
$$

Expomential growth

  • How many possible configurations?
  • Not all of them are feasible.
  • How many are they? $2^{|I|}$
  • How much time to explore them all? more than $10^{10}$ centuries

Dynamic Programming

Basic principle:

  • divide and conquer
  • bottom up computation

  • Basic conventions and notations

    • assume that $I = {1,2, ‚Ä¶ , n}$

    • $O(k,j)$ denotes the optimal solution to the knapsack problem with capacity k and items [1, ‚Ä¶, j]
      $$
      maximize \sum_{i \in I}v_ix_i \
      subject\quad to \sum_{i \in I}w_ix_i \leq K \
      x_i \in {0,1} \quad (i\in 1..j)
      $$

  • We are interested in finding out the best balue $O(K,n)$

Recurrence Relations (Bellman Equations)

  1. We know how to solve $O(k,j-1)$ for all $k$ in $0 ‚Ä¶ K$.
  2. We only consider one more item $j$ to solve $O(k,j)$
  3. In summary,

$$
O(k,j)=max(O(k,j-1), v_i+O(k-w_j, j-1)) \quad if ; w_j \leq k \
O(k,j) = O(k,j-1) \quad otherwise
$$

  1. Of course,

$$
O(k,0) = 0 \quad for ; all ; k
$$

relaxation, branch and bound

  • Iterative 2 steps
    • branching: split the problem to subproblems
    • bounding: find an optimistic estimate of the best solution to the subproblems
      • maximization: upper bound
      • minimization: lower bound

Linear Relaxition

$$
let ; x_i = \frac{y_i}{v_i} \
maximize \sum_{i \in 1..j}y_i \
subject ; to \sum_{i \in 1..j} \frac {w_i} {v_i}y_i \leq K \
0 \leq y_i \leq 1 ; (i \in 1..j)
$$
